Ephemeris · Issue 032 03 / 7 · Google
vol. xxvi · no. 5 · science & tools

Read the literature in your sleep.

Google folded three prototypes under one label: Co‑Scientist for hypothesis generation, AlphaEvolve and ERA for computational discovery, and Literature Insights via NotebookLM. The bundle plugs into Antigravity's Skills system with native handles for UniProt and AlphaFold.

Hypothesis
Co-Scientist proposes; you adjudicate.
Discovery
AlphaEvolve + ERA search the program space.
Synthesis
NotebookLM digests the corpus, cites primary.
Validated
Two papers shipped in Nature the same week.

For the working scientist the press release is the wrong primitive — what matters is whether the hypothesis you get back at 9 a.m. is one a peer reviewer would entertain. Google is betting the lab notebook is the unit that gets agentic first.

The handles into UniProt and AlphaFold do most of the load-bearing here. Skills attach domain databases to the tool-call layer; the model stops bluffing about a protein it has never seen and looks up the structure. The literature-insights piece, meanwhile, lets a postdoc walk into a new field with a 30-minute brief that names the open questions and the cliques that hold them.

None of this replaces the bench. It replaces the part of the bench that involves reading PDFs at 11 p.m.
